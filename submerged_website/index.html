the submerged state a project by christian finlay
the "submerged state" : government programs and policies that provide benefits to citizens in obscured, invisible ways so that many recipients don't realize the assistance comes from public social spending.
the nature of submerged policies limits public awareness and debate over program effectiveness, reducing pressures for politicians to justify or improve assistance--sometimes obscuring who benefits from programs can enable disparities where influential groups quietly secure favors, tax breaks, and incentives for themselves through hidden provisions in the tax code or bureaucracy.
you rely on hidden support from government programs every day without even realizing it. this "submerged state" of obscured public assistance can make getting help less embarrassing, but also shields critical social policies from public scrutiny and debate about whether they work.
